Da Vinci's Artworks

Mona Lisa

Mona Lisa, also known as Portrait of Lisa Gherardini, wife of Francesco del Giocondo, Italian La Gioconda, or French La Joconde,
was painted on a poplar wood panel by Leonardo Da Vinci using oil paint.
It is currently hanging in the Louvre Museum in Paris.

Vitruvian Man

Vitruvian Man, a drawing made in about 1490. It was made accompanied by the notes of Roman Architect Vitruvius. The art was drawn on paper using ink. It is kept in the Gabinetto dei disegni e delle stampe of the Gallerie dell'Accademia, in Venice, Italy, under reference 228. It is displayed to the public only occasionally, so it is not part of the normal exhibition of the museum.

The Last Supper

The Last Supper, was worked on by Leonardo from about 1495 to 1498 but was not continuously worked on. It was commissioned as part of a renovation plan to the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan, Italy by Leonardo's patron Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan. The painting shows the scene of the Last Supper of Jesus with his apostles. Little of the original painting remains today due to a variety of environmental factors, intentional damage and the methods used to restore it. The last restoration attempt was in 1999.

Baptism of Christ

Baptism of Christ,is a painting finished around 1475 in the studio of the Italian Renaissance painter Andrea del Verrocchio and his pupil Leonardo Da Vinci. John the Baptist is clothed in robes with a halo above his head while barefoot in the river. There are two angels kneeling and holding Jesus's garment, and the other with its hands folded, both in front of the symbolisation of salvation and life, the palm tree. Jesus has a halo over his head as he is depicted praying barefoot in the river.
