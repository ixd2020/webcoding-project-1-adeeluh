Who is Leonardo Da Vinci?

Leonardo Da Vinci was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance. He was considered one of the greatest painters of all time.
His work, Mona Lisa, was the most famous of his works and the most famous portrait ever made. His fame initially rested on his achievements as a painter.
However, he also became known for his notebooks, in which he made drawings and notes on science and invention; these involved a variety of subjects including anatomy, astronomy, botany, cartography, painting, and palaeontology.
Leonardo's genius epitomized the Renaissance humanist idea, and his collective works compose a contribution to later generations of artists rivalled only by that of his contemporary Michelangelo.
